# Providing Feedback and Reporting Issues for Saiku

Your feedback and issue reports are invaluable to the continuous improvement of Saiku. This guide outlines how to effectively provide feedback and report issues, ensuring they are addressed promptly and accurately.

## Why Your Feedback Matters

- **Product Improvement**: Your feedback helps us understand what works well and what can be improved in Saiku.
- **Community Engagement**: Sharing your experiences encourages a collaborative environment where everyone's voice is heard.

## How to Provide Feedback

- **Community Forums**: Engage in community discussions, offering suggestions and sharing your experiences.
- **User Surveys**: Participate in surveys that we periodically release to gather user insights.
- **Direct Communication**: Reach out via email or social media platforms to share your thoughts and experiences.

## Reporting Issues

If you encounter a bug or an issue in Saiku, reporting it helps us address the problem effectively.

### Steps for Reporting Issues:

1. **Check Existing Issues**: Before reporting, check if the issue has already been reported.
2. **Provide Detailed Information**:
   - Describe the issue clearly and concisely.
   - Include steps to reproduce the issue, if applicable.
   - Specify the version of Saiku you're using.
   - Attach screenshots or logs if they can help clarify the issue.
3. **Use the GitHub Issue Tracker**: Report the issue using the [Saiku GitHub Issues](https://github.com/saiku/saiku/issues) page.

## Feature Requests

Have ideas for new features or enhancements? We'd love to hear them!

- **Submit a Feature Request**: Use the GitHub Issues page to submit feature requests.
- **Detail Your Proposal**: Explain how the feature would work and why it would be beneficial.
- **Engage in Discussions**: Be open to discussions and feedback from the community and the Saiku team.

<Note>
  Your feedback and issue reports play a crucial role in shaping Saiku's development. We are committed to listening and responding to our community's needs.
</Note>
